Selected Publications

Accessibility, Making and Tactile Robotics: Facilitating Collaborative Learning and Computational Thinking for Learners with Visual Impairments

This poster focuses on accessibility concerns that learners with visual impairments (LVIs) face in making environments, particularly with contemporary toolkits. This exploratory study was conducted over a three-day summer making workshop with visually impaired high school students to explore some major challenges and potentials of tangible making and robotics platforms, utilizing KIBO as a model. We explored how a tangible coding platform (KIBO) and accessible design modifications affected individual and collaborative group interaction and cognition.
JooYoung Seo, Gabriela T. Richard
In ICLS, 2018

The Intersections of Equity, Inclusivity and Accessibility in Game-Based Learning: Proposing a Tripartite Framework for Research and Design

While there have been notable efforts to increase gender equity and cultural inclusivity in game-based learning, the addition of dis/ability has had less prominence, and, when it has been included, the focus has been on impairments instead of a social framing of dis/ability. Through a critical review of intersectionality, accessibility and universal design in related research and practice, we propose that intersectional research approaches and design practices better address equitable, inclusive and accessible design goals, which have overlapping qualities. We propose an intersectionality-informed tripartite framework, applied through related work, that integrates multiple modalities, inclusive and equitable practices, and participatory or adaptive approaches to address complementary areas of inequitable learning and design across dis/ability, gender, ethnicity and cultural background.
Gabriela T. Richard, JooYoung Seo
In BJET, 2018

Embracing Learners with Visual Impairments in CSCL

This autoethnographic study aims to shed light on the collaborative experience of a learner with a visual impairment in a blended CSCL environment. Through the analysis of personal reflections, this qualitative study captures some emergent themes and challenges in both the face-to-face and virtual environments with the intent to improve diversity, accessibility, and equity in CSCL.
JooYoung Seo, Mona AlQahtani, Xueying Ouyang, Marcela Borge
In CSCL, 2017
